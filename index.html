Public Intellectual · Symbolic Jurist · Mythopoeist

Robert Rosina is a symbolic jurist and mythopoeist who explores how mythology shapes law and power. His interdisciplinary work draws from comparative mythology, legal philosophy, literary theory, and ancient history. As a public intellectual, he examines the deep structures that underlie cultural authority, with a focus on Indo-European traditions including Norse, Celtic, Greek, and Vedic cosmologies, bridging academic theory with cultural commentary. His academic interests include sacred legal traditions such as oaths, divine justice, and metaphysical order, and the study of Indo-European cultures and their languages.

Admitted to the Supreme Court of New South Wales, Robert holds a Juris Doctor with Distinction from Macquarie University—where he received the Highest Achiever award in both Alternative Dispute Resolution and Health Law—and a Bachelor of Media with Distinction, a background that informs his understanding of narrative construction.

He is the creator of Wyrdcasters, a fictional universe rooted in Indo-European cosmology, and the author of the forthcoming novel Wyrcasters: Chaos in a Dying World.
